## Custom Builds

If none of the three flagships fit, we build you something. The **wildlife tour packages** below are the most common custom requests we receive, and each one starts with the same conversation: what do you actually want, and how many nights do you have?

### Tadoba Weekend (2 to 3 nights)

The Mumbai and Pune weekend escape. Fly to Nagpur on Friday evening, drive 3 hours, two full days of game drives, fly home Sunday or Monday. Open terrain, bold tigers, the highest sighting rate of any park within weekend reach of a major Indian city.

### Multi-Park Luxury (7 to 10 nights)

The flagship-plus version. Same Central Indian arc as the Tiger Trail, but in the lodges where you do not pick the room because every room is the right room. The naturalists are the same; the difference is the bed at the end of the day and the food at the start of it. The most popular **wildlife safari packages india** request among our repeat international guests.

### Golden Triangle + Ranthambore (8 to 10 days)

Delhi, Agra, Jaipur, Ranthambore. The classic India circuit with the wildlife chapter that the standard version skips. The Taj at sunrise, then the fort at Agra, then Jaipur, then five hours south to a tigress walking through tenth-century ruins. The most-requested of all our **india wildlife tour packages** from first-time visitors to India.

### Northeast Explorer (6 to 8 nights)

Kaziranga and Manas. Rhinos in herds, wild elephants in larger herds, and birds in numbers that make a casual birder reconsider their hobby. The trip for guests who have already done the Central Indian parks and want a completely different landscape.

### Family Safari (4 to 5 nights)

Pench or Kanha, occasionally both. Lodges that are good with children. Naturalists who know how to make the forest exciting for a six-year-old without losing the adults. Age 6 and up works well.

### Walking and Boat Safari (Satpura, 4 to 5 nights)

The trip nobody else at your dinner party will have done. Walking safaris in the morning. Boat safaris on the Denwa river. Lower tiger sighting rate than the famous parks, which is the entire point. Sloth bears, gaur, leopards, and the kind of forest you walk through on foot.

## Quick Answers

### Can I customise these packages?

Yes. They are starting points, not finished products. Every trip we run is built around the guest. The flagship packages are the ones we build often enough that they have their own pages. The rest gets built from scratch.

### What is the best time for a tiger safari in India?

October to June for most parks. Peak sightings are March to May, when the heat pushes tigers to water. November to February is more comfortable but the sightings are slightly less concentrated. Both are good. The choice is between cool weather and the highest possible sighting probability.

### Children?

Age six and up works well in most parks. Younger children can come, but the 5:30 AM wake-up calls are harder than parents expect, and the long drives between sightings can be a challenge for them. Pench and Kanha are the most family-friendly parks.

### Multiple parks in one trip?

Our speciality. The Central India Tiger Trail does three. The custom builds can do up to four or five if you have the time. The geography matters: parks within driving distance of each other (Kanha to Bandhavgarh, or Tadoba to Pench) are much easier than parks that require a flight in between.

### Packing?

We send a detailed packing list once you have booked. The short version: khaki, olive, brown. Layers for cold mornings. Comfortable walking shoes. A camera with a zoom if you want photographs. No bright colours.

### What are tiger safari packages in India usually priced at?

Across the industry, **tiger safari packages india** vary enormously based on park, lodge tier, season, and duration. We do not publish ranges on this page because every range we have ever seen quoted has been wrong for some segment of guests. We send a written, itemised quote within 24 hours of an enquiry, and the quote is detailed enough that you can compare it line by line against any other operator. The variable that matters most is rarely the lodge price. It is the quality of the guide, and that does not always correlate with what you pay.
