## What Happens Next

The **tiger safari booking** process with us is built around a written quote you can actually compare against other operators, not a phone call you have to take a decision on.

### 1. We read your enquiry

Usually within a few hours of you sending it, always within 24. If anything is unclear, we send a quick reply to ask, rather than guessing.

### 2. We send you a written quote

Itemised. Lodge by lodge, drive by drive, park by park, transfer by transfer. No hidden fees and no asterisks. The quote is detailed enough that you can compare it line by line against any other quote you have received.

### 3. You ask for changes

This is the most common step, and it is the part of **safari booking in India** that most operators skip. You will probably want to swap a lodge, add a day, change a park, or move the dates. We expect this. We will revise the quote until it actually fits the trip you want, and we will keep revising it until you are sure.

### 4. You confirm and we lock the trip

Once you say yes, we lock the permits and the lodge rooms. You pay a 30% deposit. The final balance is due 30 days before departure. From that point on, the trip is fixed and the only thing left for you to do is turn up at the airport.

There is no deposit at any point before you say yes. You can ask as many questions as you want without commitment. The team that handles the enquiry is the team that runs the trip, so you are not getting passed between sales and operations.

## A Few Common Questions

### Is there a deposit at the enquiry stage?

No. There is no payment of any kind until you have confirmed an itinerary in writing. The quote, the revisions, and the planning conversation are all free.

### How quickly will I hear back?

Within 24 hours, almost always faster. If you submit on a weekend or a holiday, the first reply might come on the next working day.

### Can I send a **safari enquiry in India** without committing to dates?

Yes. About a third of our enquiries arrive without firm dates. We send back rough pricing for two or three likely scenarios and then narrow down once you decide.

### Do you handle **tiger reserve booking** as a standalone service, separate from the lodge?

Not usually. The permits, the lodge, and the guide are tightly linked, and separating them tends to produce worse trips at higher prices. We bundle everything because that is how the trip actually works on the ground.
